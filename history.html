History of Opposite Day

Learn the history of the day of opposites

The Beginnings


Opposite Day was made popular by President Dwight Eisenhower to please his granddaughter, but he only set it as one day. He declared it to take place on August 17th. Though it is celebrated nationally on January, 25th, some people argue that it is supposed to take place on January 7th.

This holiday is an anti-holiday because it can be celebrated on every day except the day it takes place. Opposite day is a paradox. If everything is opposite, then the day of opposites is an opposite, making it not the day of opposites. This doesn't make sense, but that's okay.

Opposite Day in the modern world


January 25th, Opposite Day, has appeared in multiple forms of media over the years, and has become a well known event throughout generations. Appearing in children's shows such as Spongebob Squarepants, Opposite Day has been taught to younger audiences, which they spread to others through games.

While not everyone agrees on a date, people sometimes make jokes about the day, saying that it is Opposite Day to get their way when dealing with things.
